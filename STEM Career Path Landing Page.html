The opportunity

A degree opens the door. AI-era readiness walks through it.

STEM graduates now need practical projects, AI-assisted workflows, cloud exposure, professional branding, and clear career direction. This program helps students move from uncertainty to a structured readiness path.

Career Uncertainty

Graduates don't know where to start, what employers value most, or how to position themselves in an AI-shifted job market.

Skills Gap

Classroom theory rarely covers cloud platforms, AI workflows, and the everyday tooling that modern teams expect from day one.

Portfolio Gap

Hiring managers want evidence of shipped work. Most graduates have coursework — not deployed, demonstrable projects.

Access Gap

Mentorship, referrals, and feedback are unevenly distributed. We open the door wider through volunteer mentors and community projects.

The 8 Pillars

A readiness pathway, not a checklist

Follow the S-curve trail through eight reinforcing pillars — each one builds on the last toward a portfolio of real skills, real projects, and real career options.

1

AI-Augmented Skills

Use AI tools responsibly for research, coding support, writing, debugging, and career preparation.

Example: Build a study workflow with Claude and Cursor to cut review time in half.
2

Deployed Project Portfolio

Build and publish real projects that demonstrate practical skills — not just classroom theory.

Example: Ship a full-stack app to AWS, with a public repo and demo link.
3

Gig Economy Entry

Learn how Upwork, Contra, and Toptal work, and how to position small technical services professionally.

Example: Land a first freelance brief, scoped and priced with mentor review.
4

Personal Branding

Improve your LinkedIn, GitHub, resume, portfolio, and professional storytelling.

Example: Rewrite your LinkedIn headline and pin three repos that tell a story.
5

Micro-Internships

Gain short, focused project experience that helps you build confidence and work samples.

Example: Complete a 2–4 week nonprofit sprint with a real deliverable.
6

Strategic Certifications

Identify practical certifications from AWS, Google, Microsoft, Databricks, or other relevant platforms.

Example: Plan an AWS Cloud Practitioner path mapped to your portfolio.
7

Industry Tooling

Practice tools modern teams expect: GitHub, cloud platforms, automation, documentation, AI assistants.

Example: Set up CI/CD, issue templates, and a clean README before review.
8

Community Impact Projects

Contribute to mission-driven Code For Good projects that support nonprofits and local communities.

Example: Join a small team building a tool that saves a nonprofit 20% of their time.
How it works

From sign-up to job-ready in five clear steps

A simple participant journey designed to meet you where you are — whether you're in school or recently graduated.

  1. 1

    Sign up & share

    Tell us your background, target roles, and current readiness level.

  2. 2

    Follow the 8 pillars

    Work the readiness pathway with structured milestones and check-ins.

  3. 3

    Build & ship

    Develop deployable projects and refine your career materials in parallel.

  4. 4

    Get mentor feedback

    Receive resume reviews, project critique, and mock interviews from volunteers.

  5. 5

    Launch your path

    Prepare for jobs, freelance work, internships, or startup paths — your call.

Two tracks, one pathway

Choose the timeline that fits your stage

Whether you're still in school or sprinting toward your first role, there's a structured track shaped for you.

12–18 months

During School Track

For current high school, college, or graduate students who can build readiness over multiple academic terms.

Main outcomes

  • Build AI-augmented learning habits
  • Complete portfolio projects gradually
  • Strengthen GitHub, LinkedIn, and resume presence
  • Explore certifications strategically
  • Gain confidence before graduation

Suggested milestones

  1. Foundation and self-assessment
  2. AI-era skill building
  3. Portfolio project development
  4. Certifications and industry tooling
  5. Micro-internships or volunteer projects
  6. Career materials and interview prep
8–12 weeks

Recent Graduate Track

For students who have graduated or are close to graduation and need a focused career-readiness sprint.

Main outcomes

  • Clarify target roles quickly
  • Polish resume, LinkedIn, and GitHub
  • Build or improve one deployable project
  • Practice interview and job-search workflows
  • Job, freelance, internship, or startup ready

Suggested milestones

  1. Career direction and readiness audit
  2. Resume, LinkedIn, and GitHub cleanup
  3. Portfolio project sprint
  4. AI and industry tooling practice
  5. Applications and mock interviews
  6. Final readiness review with mentor
