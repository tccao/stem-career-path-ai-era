Tech for Nonprofits. Skills for Change.

Empowering nonprofits through technology to transform lives and communities. From our beginnings as a hackathon, we’ve grown into a volunteer-driven nonprofit delivering scalable, cost-effective tech solutions.

Our organization is built on 3 main pillars:
1. Our fellow non profit organizations that serve various communities. We aim to save their day to day work atleast by 20% through digitization
2. Our Volunteers who are students or women in sabbatical looking for a break in the tech inustry or getting into their dream schools having a safe playground to learn and contribute as well as get visibility through referrals and mock interviews.
3. The beneficiaries of the non profit organizations we digitize get to train as tier 1 support for the applications we build and get absorbed as tech support by the very non profits that served them.

Our diverse team of professionals, students, and returnees dedicate their time to help nonprofits reclaim manual hours through automation and AI-driven solutions.

About Us

Code For Good is an accredited non-profit organization, established in September 2024, dedicated to empowering fellow non-profits through digital transformation and AI adoption. Our mission is to help these organizations save up to 20% of their time, enabling them to focus on what truly matters—serving their communities.

Our network includes technologists, students, and professionals re-entering the workforce who volunteer to make an impact. We not only develop and implement software solutions but also empower underprivileged youth to manage and maintain these systems—building valuable STEM skills for their future.

Success Stories

Safe AI for Kids

Code For Good is developing a child-safe AI literacy program that teaches kids how to interact responsibly with AI tools. The initiative focuses on age-appropriate digital citizenship, helping children understand AI concepts, recognize bias, and use technology ethically—partnering with schools and youth-serving nonprofits to bring the curriculum directly into classrooms and after-school programs.

Volunteers: Esha Kaur Mander Roshni Konanki Rohan Konanki Anshita tatavarty

STEM Graduates Career Path — AI Era

Code For Good's STEM Career Readiness Program is a structured, self-paced initiative designed to close that gap. Built on 8 pillars: i) AI-augmented skills ii) a deployed project portfolio iii) gig economy entry (Upwork, Contra, Toptal) iv) personal branding v) micro-internships vi) strategic certifications (AWS, Google, Databricks) vii) industry tooling, and viii) community impact projects. The program runs as a 12–18 month track for current students and an 8–12 week speed track for recent graduates. Students who complete it, become employable,optioned: ready for a job offer, freelance income, or a startup of their own.

Agentic AI for Nonprofit Organizations

Code For Good is pioneering the adoption of Agentic AI workflows for small and mid-sized nonprofits that lack the resources to hire dedicated tech staff. Using platforms like n8n, we design and deploy AI agent pipelines that automate grant research, donor outreach, reporting, and operations empowering mission driven organizations to scale their impact without scaling their overhead.

Volunteers: Rohan Koditala, Sravan Namuduri

MesaFarm

In 2025, Code For Good volunteers built an end-to-end automation system for MesaFarm, a nonprofit serving homeless youth aged 18–25. The platform includes admin, volunteer, and participant portals built with Flutter, AWS Amplify, and Node.js with a 0-1 automation

Volunteers: Harsha Pedada, Rohan Koditala, Sannihith Ravulapalli, Sravan Namuduri

For The Need Foundation

Code For Good is helping For The Need Foundation secure and automate their mentorship platform using Azure, Microsoft 365, and AI tools for full operations automation including profanity detection and data protection on their real time chat module. The project is progressing toward full Agentic AI-driven automation using n8n platform for AI agent workflow orchestration.

Volunteers: Harshini Valluri, Amrutha Varshini, Asha Dasi, Sravan Namuduri, Rohan Koditala, Balaji Varadachari

Hackathon 2024

In December 2023, Big Brothers Big Sisters of Ventura County, CEO Danica Selvaggio approached Code For Good for help with their youth tracking process in the Youth centers. Our volunteers built a secure, mobile-friendly Flutter app on AWS Amplify with a Node.js backend and MySQL database, integrating image recognition and automated notifications to enhance engagement and data security. This initiative lead Founder, Saritha Ivaturi to incorporate Code For Good Non Profit organization in 2024.

Mentorship Initiatives

Code For Good mentors women re-entering the workforce and underprivileged students applying to college—offering free support for resumes, essays, and project-based learning as well as financial aid. Several mentees have been accepted into top U.S. universities.

Featured Projects by high school students: Viz-AI-Learn, CallWiz
